About Me

I'm a Research Technician in Mike White's lab at UGA, where we research sex chromosome evolution and explore what role genetic recombination plays in this process. I'm currently assisting Dr. White with his work in assembling a Y reference sequence for the threespine stickleback; I'm also helping to identify potential Y chromosome gene conversions in Japan Sea stickleback individuals. In addition to these projects that utilize molecular approaches, I'm using computational techniques to compare PAR boundaries between stickleback populations.

I graduated from UGA in May 2015, majoring in Genetics with a certificate in Computer Science. I'm interested in evolutionary genomics.
