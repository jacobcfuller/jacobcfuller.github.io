About Me

I'm a Research Technician in Dr. Mike White's lab at UGA, where we research sex chromosome evolution and what role genetic recombination plays in this process. I'm currently exploring Y chromosome variation in threespine stickleback by analyzing next gen sequences, utilizing Python scripts and available genomics software.



I graduated from UGA in May 2015, majoring in Genetics with a Certificate in Computing. The skills I have developed from my education, research, and job experience have led me to seek software developer positions where I can leverage the analytical, detail-driven mindset I've acquired.

When I'm not troubleshooting my Python scripts, I enjoy spending time playing music with my friends, cycling, and backpacking.
